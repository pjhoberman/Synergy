Appraisal is a technical specialty. Without specific functional training staff responsible for the appraisal function may not be fully aware of the significant financial costs and severe reputational risks associated with non-compliance. We develop and deliver customized training for agents, loan officers, origination managers, processors, risk managers and underwriters to empower them with a clear understanding of how to comply with the new appraiser independence regulations in Dodd-Frank. Our training presentations are well-received and provide valuable professional development opportunities at no charge.

Unless an institution has a specialized functional team to administer, assign, communicate, compensate and manage appraisers, it may unintentionally incur compliance violations. In addition, unless a lending institution has review appraisers on staff, it is unlikely to be effective at evaluating appraisal quality and compliance as required by Dodd-Frank's new Mandatory Reporting provision.

The Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act (H.R. 4173) includes "Appraiser Independence Requirements" (Section 1472, Subsection 129E). These new regulations replace the HVCC and are much more comprehensive. Section 1472 includes strong prohibitions against attempts to unduly influence appraisers, clear definitions regarding appropriate communication protocols, strict requirements for managing appraisers and the appraisal process, and two controversial provisions: Mandatory Reporting and Customary and Reasonable Fee.

Since Dodd-Frank includes regulations implemented by many federal agencies—such as the FDIC, OCC, NCUA, and BCFP—and significant amendments to TILA, FIRREA, RESPA and ECOA, it is imperative that clients understand the best ways to ensure compliance with these far-reaching regulatory changes.
